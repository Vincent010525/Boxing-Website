Mike Tyson

Career

Mike Tyson started of his boxing career after having a rough and criminal childhood. He got discovered by his trainer and father figure, Cus D'Amato, who trained him to become a ferocious fighter. He would start his professional career at 18 years old, after having a successfull amateur career. He ended up knocking out almost all of his opponent in the beginning of his career, many in the first round. When he was only 20 years old, he was undefeated with 27 wins, and won the heavyweight title as the youngest person ever. He then won some more fights before finally losing to Buster Douglas. He managed to comeback from this loss and won even more fights, before he eventually would spend 4 years in prison due to a rape conviction. Many people think that getting sent to prison, along with having his father figure trainer Cus D'amato pass away when he was 19, greatly hurt Tyson's boxing career. He came back to boxing with a few wins after getting out of prison, even becoming heavyweight champion again. His reign as champion was short lived due to Evander Holyfield, who ended up beating Tyson twice, something that no other fighter ever did. Tyson's career was pretty much over after this point, with him only winning 5 fights after the losees to Holyfield, while losing 4 more fights.

Early Career

Mike Tyson was born in 1966, and had quite the rough childhood. He never really knew his father, and his mother was an alcoholic and a drug addict who wasn't the best of parents. Tyson has told in interviews that she used to give him alcohol and weed to make him fall asleep when he was only 8 years old, and he would start drinking alcohol and using drugs outside of the home too. He would also head on a criminal path, comitting robberies and fighting etc. He was also bullied a lot, and was forced to fight a lot to defened himself, sometimes even against adults while he himself was a young kid. At 13 years old, Tyson's criminal ways had caught up to him, and he was locked up in a juvenile facility. It was there that he got noticed by a former boxer, who introduced him to the legendary boxing trainer Cus D'Amato. D'Amato recognized the potential in Mike Tyson, as he was incredibly big, strong, fast, and just overall athletic, especially for a 13 year old boy. He took on Tyson as a boxing student, and even later became his legal guardian when Tyson's mother passed away, and had Tyson stay at his home. From age 13 to 19, Tyson dedicated his life to boxing and to Cus D'Amato who considered Tyson to be his golden student who was destined for greatness. Tyson had a great amateur career, with many dominating victories, before turning pro at 18 years old, in 1985. His dominating victories continued in his professional career, with the first 19 fights of his career being knockout wins for Tyson, and 12 of them being first round knockouts. He would win 8 more fights after this before finally getting the chance to fight for the heavyweight title.

Youngest Heavyweight Champion

In 1986, when Mike Tyson was only 20 years old, he fought for the heavyweight title against Trevor Berbick, who was famous for beating Muhammad Ali in his final fight ever. Tyson, like ususal, ended up dominating the fight, knocking Berbick out in the second round, and becoming the youngest heavyweight champion ever. Tragically, however, Cus D'Amato who was practically Mike Tyson's father, never got to see Tyson become champion since he died a year earlier. Tyson had continued his boxing career after the death of his coach and father, and he would continue his career after becoming champion, but the loss of his mentor combined with becoming extremely famous at such a young age affected him a lot, and many fans speculate that if Cus D'Amato didn't die, Tyson's career would have been even greater than it was. But back to Tyson being heavyweight champion, he would defend his title 9 times from 1987 to 1989. In classic Tyson fashion, he knocked out 7 out of his 9 opponents, 2 of them being Larry Holmes, who was a former heavyweight champion, and Frank Bruno, who later on would become the heavyweight champion in 1995.

First Loss and Prison

In the beginning of 1990, Mike Tyson was still undefeated heavyweight champion with 37 victories. He would then go on to fight Buster Douglas in February of 1990, and Mike Tyson was a massive favorite as usual, having knocked out his 6 previous opponents. Douglas had famously promised his dying mother that he would beat Tyson and become the heavyweight champion, which showed in the fight as he fought relentlessly to win the fight, even getting up from a massive Mike Tyson punch which knocked him down, and would have knocked out most fighters. Douglas got back up, however, and fought back like crazy. In the 10th round, Buster Douglas knocked out Mike Tyson against all odds, leaving Tyson with his first ever loss. After the loss, Tyson had 4 more fights in 1990 and 1991, all of which he won. He was still not the heavyweight champion though, since he hadn't got the opportunty to fight for the title that he lost to Douglas yet. After his last fight in 1991, Tyson was 24 years old, and had a record of 41 wins and 1 loss. He was about to get the opportunity to fight for the heavyweight title again, but then he got accused of rape, and got convicted to 6 years in prison. Tyson always denied the allegations, even after being convicted, and many people to this day believe that he was falsely accused and convicted. Regardless if he was actually guilty or not, his time in prison where he had no opportunity to fight, or even train boxing, severely hurt his career.

Prison Release and Comeback

After being in prison for 4 years, Mike Tyson was released and immediately returned to boxing, fighting Peter McNeeley in 1995. Tyson won in the first round, proving that he was still a real threat in the ring. Although he couldn't do any boxing training in jail, he did a lot of weightlifting and was even bigger and stronger than when he had went into prison. After beating McNeeley, Tyson won 3 more fights, one of them being against Frank Bruno in 1996, who he had beaten already, and who was now the heavyweight champion. Later in 1996, Tyson would fight Evander Holyfield, who ended up being one of his biggest rivals. Holyfield was a smaller guy who previously had fought in a lighter weight division, but later on ended up going up to heavyweight. He was a very skilled boxer, but most people expected Tyson to defeat him and defend his heavyweight title. Holyfield showed his skills in the fight against Tyson, and ended up beating him, stripping Tyson of his heavyweight title. One year later in 1997, Tyson got the rematch against Holyfield to try and win back the heavyweight title. The fight would only last 3 rounds, and during those 3 rounds Holyfield fought very dirty, headbutting Tyson multiple times. This angered Tyson so much that he ended up biting of Holyfield's ear, which obviously resulted in Mike Tyson being disqualified. After this fight, Tyson never really returned to the great boxer that he once was. He would win 4 more fights from 1999 to 2001, before fighting Lennox Lewis in 2002. Lewis knocked him out in the 8th round. In 2003, Tyson beat Clifford Etienne, making his record an impressive 50 wins and 4 losses. He would, however, end up fighting 2 more times before finally retiring in 2005. Tyson unfortunately lost both of these fights. In 2025, when Tyson was 58 years old, he made a surprising return to boxing, with a fight against Jake Paul, who was a 27 year old youtuber. The fight was very controversial due to the massive age difference, and to no surprise, Tyson ended up losing the fight.
